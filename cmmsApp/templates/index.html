Voltage Levels

The Three Categories of Electrical Switchgear

Low Voltage (LV) Switchgear

LV Switchgear handles voltages up to 1 kV. This category is crucial for the final power distribution, control, and protection within residential, commercial, and industrial facilities.

  • Primary Use: Final distribution and control at the point of consumption.
  • Components: Includes MCCBs, MCBs, contactors, and relays.
  • Safety Criticality: Essential for personnel safety and equipment protection at the application level.

Medium Voltage (MV) Switchgear

Medium-voltage switchgear is available in classes ranging from 3.3 kV to 33 kV. Its main purpose is to distribute electrical energy to various electrical networks within a large facility or substation.

  • Key Functionality: Switching on and off, short circuit current interruption, and switching capacitive/inductive currents.
  • Equipment Types: Includes minimum oil, bulk oil, air magnetic, SF6 gas-insulated, and vacuum circuit breakers.
  • Requirement: All functions must be performed with a high level of safety and dependability.

High Voltage (HV) Switchgear

High voltage refers to the power system that deals with voltages more than 36 kV. Due to the high voltage level, the arcing created during the switching operation is significantly high, requiring specialized construction.

  • Major Component: High voltage circuit breakers (e.g., SF6 circuit breakers or vacuum circuit breakers ).
  • Design Focus: Requires unique characteristics for safe and reliable functioning to handle extreme arcing and voltage.
  • Operation: Most of the time, these circuit breakers remain in the ON position , necessitating extreme long-term reliability for when operation is finally needed.
